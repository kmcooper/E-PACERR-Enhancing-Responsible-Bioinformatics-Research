Statistical Tests and Assumptions Quiz

Identify what each statistical test measures and its key assumptions.

← Back to Module  |  Back to all Modules

1. What does a t-test measure?


Correct: t-tests compare means between two groups.
2. Which assumption is required for a t-test?


Correct: t-tests assume approximate normality and independent observations.
3. What does ANOVA test?


Correct: ANOVA compares means across multiple groups.
4. Which assumption is important for ANOVA?


Correct: ANOVA assumes homogeneity of variance across groups.
5. What does a chi-square test measure?


Correct: chi-square evaluates relationships between categorical variables.
6. Which assumption is required for chi-square tests?


Correct: chi-square requires sufficient expected counts in contingency table cells.
7. What does correlation measure?


Correct: correlation measures linear association between two continuous variables.
8. Which assumption applies to Pearson correlation?


Correct: Pearson correlation assumes a linear relationship.
9. Which test compares paired measurements from the same subjects?


Correct: paired t-tests analyze before-after or matched samples.
10. Which assumption applies broadly to most statistical tests?


Correct: independence of observations is a core assumption across many tests.
