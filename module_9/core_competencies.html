Overview

While bioinformatics as a discipline currently does not have any official form of licensure or credentialing (beyond typical degree programs), there are certain skillsets that may be expected of a competent bioinformatician.

Core Competencies

Per Javidan 1998 as well as Pralahad and Hamel in 1990, a core competency is a collection of measurable knowledge, skills, and abilities (KSAs) that are widespread in practice and often require require collective disciplinary learning and engagement with the community. They were first described in the context of business strategy, and are therefore closely aligned with workforce development expectations. Informally, you can view core competencies as those skills, knowledge, and abilities that are expected of a trained bioinformatician in the workforce. While they are sometimes criticized for focusing on skillsets over deep understanding, they provide one metric for understanding what areas you may have strengths and weaknesses.

Some readings on core competencies in bioinformatics (that are worth a skim, at least!) can be found below:
  • Wilson Sayres, Melissa A., et al. "Bioinformatics core competencies for undergraduate life sciences education." PloS one 13.6 (2018): e0196878.
  • Mulder, Nicola, et al. "The development and application of bioinformatics core competencies to improve bioinformatics training and education." PLoS computational biology 14.2 (2018): e1005772.
  • Welch, Lonnie, et al. "Bioinformatics curriculum guidelines: toward a definition of core competencies." PLOS computational biology 10.3 (2014): e1003496.
  • Lloret-Llinares, Marta, et al. "Competencies for bioinformatics core facility scientists: extension of the ISCB competency framework for bioinformatics." Bioinformatics Advances 5.1 (2025): vbaf206.
