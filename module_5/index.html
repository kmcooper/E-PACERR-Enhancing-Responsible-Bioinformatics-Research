Module 5: Resources for Responsible Research in Bioinformatics

← Back to all modules

Learning Objectives

  • Identify key ethical, methodological, and reproducibility issues presented in bioinformatics case studies.
  • Explain how failures in replicability, transparency, or validation contributed to flawed or misleading research outcomes.
  • Evaluate a bioinformatics case study using established frameworks (e.g., reproducibility, bias, generalizability, data governance).
  • Critically assess research decisions and propose evidence-based improvements to enhance rigor, reliability, and ethical responsibility.

Module Content

This module is slightly differnt in approach than the others, and does not have a practice quiz at the end. Instead, there is a checklist for help you develop a reproducible paper at the design stage of your research.

Estimated time to complete: 5–15 minutes

Visit: The Center for Open Science

Per their website, the Center for Open Science (COS) was "founded in 2013 to start, scale, and sustain open research practices that will democratize access to research, improve inclusion of all stakeholders, enhance accountability to research integrity, facilitate the self-corrective process of science, expand transparency and sharing of all research content, and improve research rigor and reproducibility."

Spend time exploring their website, specifically taking a look at the following pages:

  • Open Science: This page provides more information on Preregistration and Registered Reports, with additional education and events linked out as appropriate.
  • Assessing Research Practice: This page provides more information on ongoing acitvities led by or partnered with the COS.
  • Events: This page provides more information on events, in-person and online, that you can attend for free or on a fee basis. You can also watch many of their talks asynchronously on Youtube.
  • Blog: This is their archive of articles that discusses their work and findings amidst current trends and challenges.

Estimated time to complete: 30–45 minutes

Visit: The Open Science Framework (OSF)

Per their website, the Open Science Framework is a free, open platform to support your research and enable collaboration. They work in collaboration with the Center for Open Science above.

If you visit their website you can make a free account to explore the capabilities it provides, including registries, preprints, and events.

Estimated time to complete: 15 minutes

Visit: ReScience C

Per their website, ReScience C is "a platinum open-access peer-reviewed journal that targets computational research and encourages the explicit replication of already published research, promoting new and open-source implementations in order to ensure that the original research is reproducible. "

Spend time exploring their website, including:

  • The original paper describing their work: Published as an initiative in December 2017, this paper from a number of multidisciplinary authors provides rationale and future of the initiative.
  • Read/Skim Recent Articles: Take a peek at some of the papers they have recently published, their layout, and description.
  • Reviewer Guidelines: This page serves as a nice guide for reviewers, but also for potential authors, to understand how others might examine and (attempt to ) use your work.

Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes

Visit: The Turing Way

Per their website, the Turing Way is intended as a handbook for data scientists to perform reproducible and ethical science.
Spend time exploring their website. This is a rich website full of options to customize based on your needs.

Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes

Visit: Bioconductor

Per their website, the Bioconductor project is intended to "develop, support, and disseminate free open source software that facilitates rigorous and reproducible analysis of data from current and emerging biological assays."

Spend time exploring their website: https://bioconductor.org/ which includes:

  • Books
  • Code
  • Vignettes (A short description or tutorial on how to use a piece of code)
  • Training and Videos
  • Events

Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes

Visit: Retraction Watch

Retraction Watch is a group of folks that track publications that have been retracted from the scientific literature, in addition to other resources and community building. Spend time exploring their website: Click here to Visit.

You can also visit their database in a searchable format: Click here to Visit.

Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes

There are a lof of Resoures. Don't Panic!

There are many resources and communities available to help you learn to perform and evaluate research in bioinformatics. You do not need to know all of these resources intimately! The best advice is to screen them for feel and familiarity and select one or two to investigagte and see if it is a good fit for your working style and research.

Estimated time to complete: 30 minutes
