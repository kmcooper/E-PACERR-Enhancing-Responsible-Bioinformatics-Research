Module Quiz

This quiz assesses your understanding of study scope, reproducibility metrics, and generalizability.

1. Why is documenting research important?


Documentation helps researchers remember reasoning, processes, and communicate work to others.
2. Which of the following is part of defining the scope of a dataset?


Scope includes dataset characteristics like sample size, measurements, instruments, and conditions.
3. Which item is part of defining the scope of code used in a project?


Code scope includes language, libraries, environment, parameters, and computational resources.
4. What does scientific rigor refer to?


Scientific rigor involves careful design, methodology, analysis, and interpretation.
5. What does robustness describe?


Robust findings remain true across multiple datasets, applications, or methods.
6. Reliability of a computational tool refers to:


Reliability measures whether software consistently performs as expected.
7. Why is repeatability often difficult in scientific research?


Repeatability requires recreating the exact experiment with the same resources and conditions.
8. What does generalizability describe?


Generalizability refers to whether results apply beyond the specific study sample.
9. What factor strongly affects whether results are generalizable?


Generalizability depends on how well the study sample represents the broader population.
10. Which statement demonstrates appropriate caution about generalizing results?


Scientific communication should avoid overstating conclusions beyond the study sample.
